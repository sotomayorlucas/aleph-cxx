\title{One Substrate: Byte-Exact Local Maintenance of a Curvature-Weighted
Graph Laplacian for Interactive Rendering and Physics}
\author{Anonymous submission \\ \small (double-blind review)}
\date{}

\begin{document}
\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
Interactive engines keep rendering and physics in separate data structures;
geometry processing reuses prefactored Laplacians but assumes fixed topology.
We present a system in which a single Ollivier--Ricci-curvature-weighted graph
Laplacian $\Delta$ is the shared substrate for rendering signals and four
physics solvers (wave, heat, frequency-domain Helmholtz acoustics, and vector
diffusion), maintained \emph{incrementally and byte-identically} under
structural double-pushout (DPO) graph rewrites. We observe that the standard
globally-formulated Ollivier--Ricci computation is not byte-reproducible under
edits---the $\W1$ transport LP's floating-point output depends on global
support size---and show that a bounded-support curvature $\kR$ ($R{=}2$)
restores \emph{exact} locality: after an edit, recomputing only a 2-hop dirty
cover derived from the DPO interface yields an operator bit-identical to a
full rebuild, by construction. Indeed, in exact arithmetic $\kR$ \emph{equals}
the global curvature---the bounded support is not an approximation but the
same optimum computed over an edit-invariant certificate. A Mayer--Vietoris
residual over the graph's flag complex certifies each localized rebuild at
runtime. On lattice and irregular triangle-mesh benchmarks the localized
rebuild recomputes a constant number of edges as meshes grow
($\mathrm{dirty}/|E| \to 0.002$), bit-exactly on every measured edit; with a
sparse assembly of the same bit-identical entries the per-edit rebuild stays
${\sim}150\times$ below a full rebuild at every measured size. Certificate
overhead starts at ${\sim}1$\,ms, and the whole engine---editor with
undo/redo included---is deterministic to the bit.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

Real-time engines treat the render representation and the physics
representation as separate worlds synchronized by ad-hoc glue, and recent
work still describes physics engines as ``representation-agnostic'' with no
native shared substrate for rendering and simulation \TODO{cite the 2026
Gaussian-splat-physics framing after PDF verification}. Geometry processing,
by contrast, has long organized many tasks around one prefactored operator
--- discrete exterior calculus~\cite{desbrun2005dec,degoes2013dec}, the heat
method~\cite{crane2013heat}, the vector heat method~\cite{sharp2019vector},
Laplacian mesh editing~\cite{sorkine2004laplacian}, and projective
dynamics~\cite{bouaziz2014projective} all ``factor once, reuse many'' --- but
on a \emph{fixed} mesh: structural change means refactoring from scratch.

We do not introduce a new curvature, a new Laplacian, or a new incremental
factorization; we show that an Ollivier--Ricci-weighted graph Laplacian can
serve as a single, \emph{dynamically-maintained} substrate for both rendering
and several physics solvers, and that under DPO structural edits this
substrate can be rebuilt locally, byte-identically, and self-certified at
runtime by a Mayer--Vietoris residual --- turning determinism into a
correctness proof for a live, editable engine.

Concretely, we contribute:
\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{An observation} (motivation, not a theorem about ORC): under
  the global $\W1$ LP formulation (Charnes perturbation over the full
  component), the \emph{computed} curvature of far-away edges drifts
  (${\sim}10^{-10}$) when a node is added anywhere in the component. The
  mathematical locality of ORC~\cite{jost2014ollivier} does not transfer to
  the computed value (Observation~\ref{obs:nonlocal} gives the exact
  injected-mass mechanism); a byte-exact engine cannot use the global
  formulation.
\item \textbf{Bounded-support $\kR$ with exact locality}: $\kR(e)$ is a pure
  function of the induced radius-$R$ ball $\ball{R}{e}$; with $R{=}2$ the
  in-ball metric restricted to the transport support coincides with the
  global metric (Lemma~\ref{lem:metric}), in exact arithmetic $\kR$ equals
  the global curvature (Lemma~\ref{lem:value}), and the localized rebuild
  after a DPO rewrite is bit-identical to a full rebuild
  (Theorem~\ref{thm:bitexact}).
\item \textbf{A runtime cohomological certificate}: a Mayer--Vietoris
  residual over the rewrite's $U/K/R$ cover of the flag complex, evaluated
  per edit in a live engine. We claim no primacy on incremental cohomology,
  bit-exact maintenance, or MV-as-certificate --- all have close prior
  art~\cite{volk2026incremental,young2026sheaf}; \S\ref{sec:related}
  delimits the claim to its defensible core: the first cohomological
  certificate acting as an \emph{external witness} guarding a
  \emph{curvature-weighted rendering/physics operator's} localized rebuild
  under \emph{DPO scene-graph edits} in a \emph{live, deterministic,
  interactive engine}.
\item \textbf{The one-substrate system}: the same cached kernel-aware
  $LDL^\top$ of $\Delta$ drives light/importance, wave, heat, Helmholtz
  acoustics (indefinite, via Bunch--Kaufman on the $k^2$-shifted copy),
  vector diffusion, and unconditionally stable implicit stepping, in a
  deterministic interactive editor with undo/redo, gated by
  TLA\textsuperscript{+}-mirrored invariants and byte-exact CI oracles.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Related work}\label{sec:related}

\paragraph*{Ollivier--Ricci curvature and its locality.}
ORC compares 1-hop transport measures~\cite{ollivier2009ricci}; its locality
is established --- Jost and Liu bound it by degrees and short-cycle
counts~\cite{jost2014ollivier}, and exact computation reduces to a local
matching problem with $\Omega(n)$ worst-case query bounds for global
formulations~\cite{niwang2023local}. Ricci flow pipelines recompute ORC only
near modified edges~\cite{ni2019community}. We claim no locality discovery:
$\kR$ is an engineering realization of known locality, chosen so the
\emph{computed} value is a pure function of an edit-invariant ball.

\paragraph*{Incremental Laplacian factor reuse.}
Local reuse of prefactored sparse systems under mesh edits is an established
line: sub-mesh factor reuse~\cite{herholz2018factor}, localized
solves~\cite{herholz2017localized}, and interactive sparse Cholesky
updates~\cite{herholz2020sparse}; dynamic Laplacian solvers exist in
theory~\cite{durfee2019dynamic}. These optimize speed with bounded residual;
none guarantees --- or aims at --- bit-identity with a from-scratch rebuild,
and none derives its update region from a rewrite calculus. Our delta is the
guarantee (Theorem~\ref{thm:bitexact}) and the DPO-derived dirty cover
(Lemma~\ref{lem:cover}).

\paragraph*{Sheaf cohomology, incrementally and as a certificate.}
Spectral sheaf theory is due to Hansen and
Ghrist~\cite{hansen2019spectral}. Volk~\cite{volk2026incremental} is the
closest algorithmic precedent for incremental cohomology maintenance, and we
do not claim priority over it. His method maintains first sheaf cohomology on
a streaming one-dimensional complex in time independent of complex size under
bounded local geometry, and --- beyond an amortized-cost result --- proves
the maintained state ``agrees exactly with batch recomputation,'' reporting
``zero measured drift'' through five million vertices; it even assembles the
sheaf Laplacian $(\delta^0)^\top\delta^0$ and localizes injected
contradictions by recomputing a single cell. Our contribution is orthogonal
in what the cohomology is \emph{for}: Volk re-verifies a maintained
cohomological quantity against its own batch value in an offline stream; our
Mayer--Vietoris residual is an external witness certifying the byte-exact
rebuild of a curvature-weighted rendering-and-physics Laplacian under DPO
scene-graph edits inside a live, deterministic editor --- a different object,
a different guarantee, a live setting.

Young~\cite{young2026sheaf} recasts program analysis as \v{C}ech cohomology
of a semantic presheaf, where $H^0$ is ``the space of globally consistent
typings'' and $H^1$ ``classifies gluing obstructions,'' with a
Mayer--Vietoris sequence supplying ``compositional obstruction counting,
enabling incremental analysis.'' Because that work already treats cohomology
as an obstruction certificate and already uses Mayer--Vietoris for
incrementality, we claim neither idea as novel in the abstract; what is new
here is the target and the setting --- an MV residual over a visibility
sheaf on a flag complex that guards a numerical operator's localized rebuild
at interactive rates.

\paragraph*{DPO rewriting in geometry; curvature-weighted Laplacians;
determinism.}
Algebraic graph transformation is classical~\cite{ehrig2006fundamentals};
topology-based geometric modelers rewrite generalized
maps~\cite{belhaouari2014jerboa}, but no prior work couples DPO rewrites to
an incremental simulation operator. Curvature-weighted graph Laplacians
appear in spectral clustering~\cite{lly2026reweight} and Ricci-flow edge
weights~\cite{ni2019community} --- network analysis, not rendering or
physics. Bit-exact determinism is standard lockstep
engineering~\cite{fiedler2015determinism,demmel2013reproblas}; our use of it
as a per-edit correctness oracle (incremental $\equiv$ full, checked
bitwise) is, to our knowledge, new in this setting.

\section{System overview}\label{sec:system}

The scene is a typed graph $G=(V,E,\tau,\alpha)$ with ten well-formedness
invariants, specified in TLA\textsuperscript{+} and mirrored token-exactly in
the implementation (a regression test parses both). Edits are DPO rewrites
--- four verified rules (\textsf{spawn\_light}, \textsf{remove\_object},
\textsf{replace\_material}, \textsf{refine\_cell}) plus editor operations
lowered to rewrites (add/delete object with cascade semantics, transactional
OBJ import). All render and simulation products are pure functions of the
graph: a lowering pass produces the raster and path-tracing scenes; the flow
layer builds $\Delta = D_w - A_w$ over the \textsf{Adjacent} 1-skeleton with
$w(e) = 1 + \max(\kappa(e), -0.95)$; the simulation layer evolves sections
(scalar and vector fields) on $\Delta$; undo/redo replays the graph.

\section{Exact locality of bounded-support curvature}\label{sec:theory}

\subsection{Setup}

For a scene graph $G$, the \emph{skeleton} $\Sigma(G)=(V,E)$ consists of the
mesh vertices (sorted, deduplicated) and the canonical \textsf{Adjacent}
edge list (sorted vertex pairs, sorted lexicographically). $d_G$ is the hop
metric on $\Sigma(G)$, $N(v)$ the skeleton neighbourhood, and for an edge
$e=(a,b)$,
$\ball{R}{e} = \{\, v : \min(d_G(v,a), d_G(v,b)) \le R \,\}$, with
$G[\ball{R}{e}]$ the labeled induced subgraph. $\mu_v$ is uniform on
$N(v)$; $\kappa(e) = 1 - \W1(\mu_a,\mu_b)/d_G(a,b)$. The \emph{computed}
$\kR$ executes: radius-$R$ BFS ball, canonical sort, induced adjacency in
canonical edge order, in-ball all-pairs BFS metric, support restriction, and
a transportation simplex with the Charnes right-hand-side perturbation
$\mu_i \mathrel{+}= \varepsilon (i{+}1)$, $\varepsilon = 10^{-12}$, over the
support of size $n_s$ --- in \texttt{f64}, single-threaded, no randomness.

\emph{Determinism assumptions (standing).} One binary, one ISA, no runtime
FP-environment changes, single-threaded evaluation. Under IEEE-754 every
operation is a deterministic function of operand bits, so a fixed
instruction sequence on identical inputs yields identical output bits (the
deterministic-lockstep setting~\cite{fiedler2015determinism}, enforced in CI
by byte-golden oracles).

\subsection{Metric and value fidelity}

\begin{lemma}[metric fidelity of the 2-ball]\label{lem:metric}
Let $e=(a,b) \in E$, $R \ge 2$, and $x,y \in N(a) \cup N(b)$. Then
$d_{G[\ball{R}{e}]}(x,y) = d_G(x,y)$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Write $B = \ball{R}{e}$, $H = G[B]$. Since $H$ is a subgraph, $d_H \ge d_G$.
For the converse, note $d_G(x,y) \le 3$ via the walk $x\,a\,b\,y$, so
$d_G(x,y) \in \{0,1,2,3\}$, and $x,y \in B_1 \subseteq B$. Take any
$G$-geodesic $P$ from $x$ to $y$. If $|P| \le 1$ its elements are in $B$.
If $P = x\,m\,y$, then $m$ is adjacent to $x \in B_1$, so $m \in B_2
\subseteq B$. If $P = x\,m_1\,m_2\,y$, then $m_1$ is adjacent to $x$ and
$m_2$ to $y$, so $m_1, m_2 \in B_2$. In every case all vertices of $P$ lie
in $B$, its edges are induced, and $P$ is an $H$-path of length $d_G(x,y)$.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[exact-value fidelity]\label{lem:value}
In exact arithmetic with the unperturbed transport LP, $\kR(e) = \kappa(e)$
for every $e \in E$ and every $R \ge 2$: the bounded support is not an
approximation.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Both formulations minimize $\sum_{ij} \pi_{ij} c_{ij}$ over plans with
marginals $\mu_a, \mu_b$, differing only in the variable index set (global
component support vs.\ ball support) and the metric ($d_G$ vs.\ $d_H$). Mass
conservation forces $\pi_{ij}=0$ off $\operatorname{supp}\mu_a \times
\operatorname{supp}\mu_b$, so both problems equal the problem posed on the
supports alone. For $R \ge 1$ the in-ball star of $a$ equals its true star
($N(a) \subseteq B_1$, and each star edge is induced), so both formulations
carry the same marginals; by Lemma~\ref{lem:metric} the costs among support
points coincide; and $d_G(a,b)=d_H(a,b)=1$.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}
Observed discrepancies $|\kR - \kappa|$ ($\le 10^{-6}$ in our test suite,
${\sim}10^{-10}$ typical) are entirely perturbation and floating-point
artifacts; model error is exactly zero.
\end{corollary}

\begin{observation}[support-size dependence of the computed
value]\label{obs:nonlocal}
The implementation perturbs the LP right-hand side by
$\mu_i \mathrel{+}= \varepsilon(i{+}1)$, $i = 1..n_s$, and
$\nu_j \mathrel{+}= \varepsilon(n_s{+}1)/2$, injecting total extra mass
$\varepsilon\, n_s(n_s{+}1)/2$ that the optimal plan must transport. The
returned value therefore depends on the support size $n_s$ and on each
support point's index. Under the global formulation $n_s$ is the component
size: inserting \emph{any} vertex or edge moves the computed value of every
edge in the component at order $\varepsilon\, n_s\!\cdot\!\mathrm{diam}$
(measured ${\sim}10^{-10}$). This does not contradict mathematical locality
(Lemma~\ref{lem:value}); it obstructs byte-exact caching. Under the ball
formulation, $n_s$ and the indices are functions of the ball alone.
\end{observation}

\subsection{Byte-exact locality and the dirty cover}

\begin{proposition}[byte-exact locality]\label{prop:local}
Fix $R \ge 1$ and the standing determinism assumptions. If
$\ball{R}{e}$ computed in $G$ and in $G'$ coincide as node-id sets and the
labeled induced subgraphs are equal, then the computed $\kR(e;G)$ and
$\kR(e;G')$ are bit-identical.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
Every intermediate artifact is a pure function of the labeled ball: the BFS
ball set is order-invariant and self-contained (every witnessing path lies
in the ball); the ball order is its sorted id list; the induced adjacency
lists inherit the canonical edge order, whose in-ball subsequence is
intrinsic to the ball; the in-ball metric, support, measures, and the
fixed-pivot perturbed simplex consume only those artifacts. Identical inputs
to a fixed \texttt{f64} instruction sequence produce identical bits. The
full step-by-step argument is in the supplemental
proofs.\footnote{\texttt{docs/paper/proofs.md} in the artifact.}
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[dirty-cover soundness]\label{lem:cover}
Let a localizable edit take $G \to G'$, let the seed set $S$ contain every
created skeleton vertex and every endpoint of a created or deleted skeleton
edge that exists in $\Sigma(G')$, and let
$D = \{ (u,v) \in E(G') : u \text{ or } v \text{ within distance } R
\text{ of } S \text{ in } \Sigma(G') \}$. Then for every
$e = (a,b) \in E(G') \setminus D$, the labeled $R$-balls of $e$ in $G$ and
$G'$ coincide; hence by Proposition~\ref{prop:local} the cached $\kR(e;G)$
is bit-identical to $\kR(e;G')$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Suppose $e \notin D$. \emph{(i) No vertex enters the ball:} if
$v \in \ball{R}{e}^{G'} \setminus \ball{R}{e}^{G}$, a $G'$-path $P$ of
length $\le R$ from $\{a,b\}$ to $v$ must contain a created edge; the
nearer endpoint $u$ of the \emph{first} created edge along $P$ is a seed,
and the prefix of $P$ shows $d_{G'}(u,\{a,b\}) \le R$, so $e \in D$ ---
contradiction. \emph{(ii) No vertex leaves:} if
$v \in \ball{R}{e}^{G} \setminus \ball{R}{e}^{G'}$, a $G$-path of length
$\le R$ must contain a deleted edge; the prefix up to the first deleted
edge contains no deleted edge, hence survives into $\Sigma(G')$ (surviving
edges keep surviving endpoints), so its endpoint $u$ is a live seed with
$d_{G'}(u,\{a,b\}) \le R$ --- contradiction. \emph{(iii) No induced edge
changes:} a created or deleted edge $(u,w)$ with $u,w$ in the common ball
puts the live seed $u$ within $R$ of $\{a,b\}$ --- contradiction. Created
isolated vertices lie in no ball; deleted vertices are covered by (ii) via
their cascaded edge deletions.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[bitwise equality of localized and full
rebuild]\label{thm:bitexact}
Let $G_0 \to G_1 \to \cdots \to G_k$ be a trace of localizable edits, with
$\Delta_0$ the full bounded build on $G_0$ and
$\Delta_t$ the localized rebuild on $G_t$ from $\Delta_{t-1}$ with the dirty
set of Lemma~\ref{lem:cover}. Then every $\Delta_t$ is bit-identical
(matrix and curvature map) to the full bounded build on $G_t$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Induction on $t$; $t{=}0$ is definitional. For the step, each skeleton edge
of $G_t$ is either dirty or a cache miss --- recomputed by the identical
primitive on the identical shared adjacency the full build uses, hence
bit-equal --- or a clean cache hit, whose stored value equals the full
build's value on $G_{t-1}$ (inductive hypothesis) and hence, by
Lemma~\ref{lem:cover} and Proposition~\ref{prop:local}, the full build's
value on $G_t$. Both paths then insert the identical map in the identical
canonical order and run the identical assembly summation, so the matrices
agree bitwise.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}
Theorem~\ref{thm:bitexact} is enforced, not just proven: continuous
integration asserts \texttt{==} on every matrix entry and curvature across
multi-edit traces, and the benchmark harness asserts it on every measured
row of both benchmark families (\S\ref{sec:eval}).
\end{remark}

\section{The runtime Mayer--Vietoris certificate}\label{sec:certificate}

Each localizable rewrite is decomposed into subgraphs $(U, K, R)$ (survivors,
interface, replaced region). The engine builds the flag complexes of $G'$,
$U$, $R$, $K$, the visibility sheaf on each, and checks the signed residual
\[
h^0(G') \;-\; \bigl( h^0(U) + h^0(R) - h^0(K) + \varepsilon_{\mathrm{sheaf}}
\bigr) \;=\; 0,
\]
where $\varepsilon_{\mathrm{sheaf}}$ is the rank of the connecting map.
A zero residual certifies the cover that Lemma~\ref{lem:cover} decomposes
over --- an external witness for the operator rebuild, evaluated per edit on
the live graph (cost: \S\ref{sec:eval}).

\section{One substrate, five consumers}\label{sec:substrate}

The renderer's importance channel reads the curvature signal; wave, heat,
and component-wise vector diffusion are explicit matvec steppers on
$\Delta$; Helmholtz acoustics solves $(\Delta - k^2 I)\varphi = s$, reusing
the cached kernel-aware $LDL^\top$ in the PSD regime and Bunch--Kaufman
factoring the shifted copy in the indefinite regime; and implicit
backward-Euler steppers factor $I + \beta\Delta$ (SPD for all
$\beta \ge 0$) --- densely via the kernel-aware $LDL^\top$ or, from the
sparse carrier, via a sparse $LDL^\top$ (Davis-style elimination tree,
deterministic natural ordering), making the implicit pipeline sparse
end-to-end: CSR assembly $\to$ $O(\mathrm{nnz})$ shift $\to$ sparse factor
$\to$ sparse solve. Unconditional stability: our tests drive $100\times$
past the explicit CFL bound, where the explicit stepper provably diverges,
and assert bounded, mass-conserving, bit-deterministic trajectories. All
consumers share the one maintained operator; none owns a private copy of
the scene.

\section{Evaluation}\label{sec:eval}

All numbers are 5-repetition medians from the deterministic benchmark
harness; every row asserts the bit-exactness of Theorem~\ref{thm:bitexact}
(the harness exits nonzero on any mismatch --- all sweeps ran green), and
every figure regenerates from committed CSVs.

\paragraph*{Lattice family.}
$R \times R$ 4-neighbour lattices, $|V|$ 64--4096, with a per-grid edit
trace (1-edge add, 2-edge add, corner add, cascade delete) threading the
previous operator exactly as the editor does.
Figure~\ref{fig:cost} (top): the full bounded rebuild grows linearly in
$|E|$ (${\sim}15$\,ms $\to$ 4.9\,s); the dense-assembly localized rebuild
stays flat while the dirty cover is constant (37--51 edges for interior
edits, 13 at the corner, \emph{at every size}) until the $O(n^2)$ assembly
dominates (knee at ${|E| \approx 2}$k, ${\sim}130$\,ms at 4k nodes,
$37\times$). The \emph{sparse}-assembly localized rebuild --- same dirty
cover, operator entries bit-identical --- removes the knee: 22--42\,ms
across grids 32--64, i.e.\ ${\sim}152\times$ over the full rebuild at 4k
nodes, with only the linear $O(V{+}E)$ skeleton scan remaining.
$\mathrm{dirty}/|E|$ falls from 0.33--0.44 to 0.0016--0.0063 and never
approaches the engine's 0.5 fallback gate beyond toy sizes.

\paragraph*{The global-formulation contrast case.}
The globally-supported build takes 6.1\,s at 64 nodes and 337\,s at 144
nodes, versus 20\,ms and 84\,ms bounded ($309\times$, $4005\times$) --- it
is unmeasurable past toy sizes, which is precisely why a byte-exact engine
needs $\kR$ (Observation~\ref{obs:nonlocal}).

\paragraph*{Irregular family.}
Face-adjacency graphs of perforated icospheres (interior degree 3, hole
boundaries 1--2, pentagonal clusters), 78--5023 faces, plus an
\texttt{--obj} mode replaying the trace on arbitrary OBJ meshes. The same
invariants hold: constant dirty cover (15--28 edges --- tighter than the
lattice's, as triangle adjacency has smaller 2-hop balls),
$\mathrm{dirty}/|E|$ down to 0.0020, bit-exact throughout. Dense-assembly
local plateaus at ${\sim}83$\,ms at 5k faces ($14\times$); sparse-assembly
local stays at 7--8\,ms (${\sim}160\times$ over the full rebuild).

\paragraph*{Factor family.}
Factoring $I + \beta\Delta$ for the implicit steppers, dense vs sparse, on
the lattices: the dense $LDL^\top$ climbs as $O(n^3)$ --- 0.4\,ms, 2.9\,s,
19.3\,s, and 261\,s at $|V| = 64$, 1024, 2304, 4096 --- while the sparse
factor takes 0.13, 7.8, 33.5, and 112.6\,ms respectively ($2317\times$ at
4k nodes); solves are $465\times$ faster (0.24\,ms vs 113\,ms). Solutions
from the two factor paths agree within $10^{-9}$ at every size (a benchmark
gate, not an observation). Per-edit implicit-stepper refactoring is
therefore interactive at every measured scale.

\paragraph*{Certificate cost.}
The MV residual is 0 on every edit of every sweep; cost ${\sim}1$\,ms at 65
nodes, ${\sim}7.7$\,ms at 257, ${\sim}53$\,ms at 577 (superlinear; the
visibility-sheaf $H^0$ over the full flag complex dominates --- localizing
the certificate is future work, though it is already viable per-edit at
editor scale).

\section{Limitations and future work}\label{sec:limits}

The sparse CSR assembly ($O(V + E\log\deg)$, entries bit-identical to the
dense path) removes the assembly bound visible as the dense knee in
Fig.~\ref{fig:cost}, and the implicit path's $I+\beta\Delta$ factors now go
through a sparse $LDL^\top$ (\S\ref{sec:eval}). What remains dense: the
Helmholtz \emph{indefinite} Bunch--Kaufman (sparse symmetric-indefinite
factorization, where pivoting fights the symbolic structure, is a research
problem of its own) and the cached component of the incremental solver that
Helmholtz reads; fill-reducing orderings are future work. One honest
floating-point caveat, encoded in our tests: matvecs \emph{across} carriers
agree to a few ulps and factor-path solves to $10^{-9}$ --- not bitwise
(contraction and elimination-order discretion); each path is individually
byte-deterministic, and the operator entries are bit-identical. The rendering-importance channel still uses the
global-support curvature (deliberately, as the contrast case). Implicit
steppers refactor $I + \beta\Delta$ per $(\Delta, \mathrm{d}t)$; factor
\emph{updates} under edits are future work. Component-wise vector diffusion
does not couple components; a connection/Hodge Laplacian is the follow-up
numerics. The certificate is global in $G'$; localizing it would remove its
superlinear cost.

\section{Conclusion}

A curvature-weighted graph Laplacian can be the one substrate an interactive
engine derives everything from --- light, waves, heat, sound, vector fields
--- provided it can be maintained under structural edits without drift. We
showed the standard global ORC formulation cannot support that (its computed
values are support-size-dependent), that a bounded-support curvature makes
locality \emph{exact} rather than approximate, that the resulting localized
rebuild is bit-identical to a full rebuild with a machine-checked proof
structure (per-edit CI equality) and a runtime cohomological witness, and
that the whole stack holds up across two benchmark families at interactive
rates. Determinism here is not netcode hygiene; it is the proof technique.
